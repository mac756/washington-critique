The Players

Key Figures

The men who shaped history — portraits via Met Museum & Library of Congress

SECRET FILE

George Washington

1732-1799

Commander-in-Chief

Led the Continental Army through brutal winters and impossible odds. Won at Trenton and Yorktown. His leadership held the army together.

Access Classified File→

Metropolitan Museum of Art / Public Domain

Benjamin Franklin

1706-1790

Diplomat & Statesman

Secured the crucial French alliance that saved the revolution. Scientist, writer, and master negotiator who convinced France to fund the war.

Metropolitan Museum of Art / Public Domain

Marquis de Lafayette

1757-1834

French General

Young French noble who defied his king to join the American cause at just 19. Became Washington's protégé and surrogate son.

Metropolitan Museum of Art / Public Domain

Baron von Steuben

1730-1794

Prussian Inspector General

Prussian officer who transformed raw farmers into disciplined soldiers at Valley Forge. His training manual and drill techniques saved the army from collapse.

Library of Congress / Public Domain

Thomas Jefferson

1743-1826

Author of Independence

Wrote the Declaration of Independence. His words gave the revolution its moral foundation and enduring legacy.

Metropolitan Museum of Art / Public Domain

Benedict Arnold

1741-1801

Traitor (Former Hero)

Brilliant tactician whose heroics at Saratoga changed the war's course. Betrayed West Point. His name became synonymous with treason.

Metropolitan Museum of Art / Public Domain

King George III

1738-1820

British Monarch

Refused to see the colonies as equals. His inflexibility and arrogance cost Britain its American empire.

Library of Congress / Public Domain
